# Governance Model

## Governance Principle

The assistant should support employee decision-making, not replace accountable human owners for high-risk decisions.

## Human-in-the-Loop Rules

Human review is required when a question involves:

- customer commitments
- legal or compliance interpretation
- HR exceptions
- security-sensitive data
- financial approvals
- policy exceptions
- unclear or missing source material

## Escalation Criteria

Escalate when:

- no approved source is found
- the answer is low confidence
- the topic is high risk
- the user intends to act externally
- the user is requesting an exception
- the answer may affect customers, employees, or regulated data

## Decision Rights

The AI assistant may:

- summarize approved internal information
- point users to relevant sources
- suggest next steps
- identify when escalation is needed

The AI assistant may not:

- approve exceptions
- make binding decisions
- override policy
- provide legal advice
- make customer commitments
- authorize spend
- process confidential data in unapproved systems

## Governance Cadence

Recommended review cadence:

- weekly review of unanswered questions
- biweekly review of escalation patterns
- monthly review of adoption and trust metrics
- quarterly governance review with business, product, legal, security, and HR stakeholders
