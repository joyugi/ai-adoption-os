# Problem Statement

A mid-size company wants to improve employee access to internal policy and process knowledge.

Employees often lose time searching across documents, asking colleagues repetitive questions, or relying on outdated information. Leadership wants to explore an AI assistant that can answer common internal questions faster.

However, the core challenge is not simply generating answers.

The real product challenge is whether employees will:

- trust the assistant appropriately
- know when to verify information
- understand when human review is required
- avoid using AI for high-risk decisions without escalation
- give feedback that improves the system
- use the assistant repeatedly in real workflow moments

## Product Problem

How might we help employees get faster answers to internal policy and process questions while ensuring appropriate trust, source visibility, human oversight, and measurable adoption?

## Why AI May Be Appropriate

AI may be appropriate because the workflow involves:

- repeated questions
- searchable knowledge
- time lost to manual lookup
- many low-to-medium risk information requests
- opportunities to route high-risk questions to human owners

## Where AI Should Not Be Used Alone

The assistant should not be the final authority for:

- legal decisions
- employee relations issues
- customer commitments
- security-sensitive data handling
- financial approvals
- policy exceptions

In these cases, the assistant should guide the user to the right source or escalation path.
